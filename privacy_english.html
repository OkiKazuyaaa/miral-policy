1. Scope

This Privacy Policy (“Policy”) applies to the handling of personal information and personal-related information by Aspira Co., Ltd. (“Aspira”) in connection with the “MIRAL” business analytics dashboard for corporate customers (the “Service”). It covers the Service admin console, our website, and our support/contact operations.

  • Contact: contact@aspira-co.jp

3. Categories of Information We Collect

We may collect the following information (some items may not constitute personal information under applicable laws):

  • Account information: email address, name, affiliation, role/permissions, authentication information, etc.
  • Technical information and logs: IP address, user agent, device information, OS/browser info, access timestamps, operation logs, error logs, signals of unauthorized access, etc.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: cookies/local storage for session maintenance, settings, and security (see “8. Cookies”).
  • Contract/billing: contact details of the contract representative, billing details, and minimal information necessary for credit decisions.
  • Inquiries: name, contact details, inquiry content, and response history.

4. Purposes of Use

  • Identity verification/authentication and permission management
  • Security (unauthorized access detection, audit logs, vulnerability response)
  • Provision, maintenance, improvement, and quality enhancement of the Service
  • Contract performance, support responses, and important notices
  • Legal compliance, dispute handling, exercise/defense of rights
  • Other purposes incidental to the above

5. Overview of Security Measures

We take necessary and appropriate measures to prevent leakage, loss, or damage of personal data and to otherwise ensure security management, including the following (overview only; details are available upon request):

  • Organizational: internal rules for personal information handling, access/permission control, log audits, and supervision of contractors
  • Human: NDAs, training, and prompt deactivation of leavers’ permissions
  • Physical: entry/exit controls, device/media management, and secure disposal
  • Technical: access controls, encryption in transit/at rest, vulnerability management, WAF/IDS/IPS
  • External environment: understanding legal frameworks of countries/regions where data is stored/processed

6. Provision to Third Parties

We do not provide personal data to third parties without the individual’s consent, except as permitted by laws and regulations.

7. Sub-processors and Overseas Processing

We may entrust handling of personal data within the scope necessary to operate the Service and supervise contractors via agreements requiring appropriate security measures. If storage/processing occurs outside Japan, information on the destination country/region and contractor’s safeguards will be considered and addressed appropriately upon request (and where cross-border transfer constitutes third-party provision, we will obtain consent or take measures required by law).

8. Cookies and Personal-Related Information

We use session cookies only for login state and security. We do not currently use analytics or advertising cookies. If introduced in the future, we will publish their types, purposes, and opt-out methods on this page or elsewhere. For personal-related information provided by third parties, we will confirm (as required by law) whether consent has been obtained by the provider and will consider, upon request, whether to obtain and use such information as personal data within Aspira.

9. Joint Use

No joint use at present. If introduced, we will disclose items, scope of joint users, purposes, and the party responsible for management.

10. Retention and Deletion

We store and manage personal data only for as long as necessary to achieve the purposes and erase or anonymize it without undue delay thereafter. Backups are overwritten/erased per our operational policies (unless legal retention obligations apply). Specific retention periods are, in principle, not publicly disclosed and are governed by our internal rules for security and business continuity.

11. Requests for Disclosure, Correction, etc.

Individuals (or authorized agents) may request disclosure, correction/addition/deletion, suspension/erasure, or cessation of third-party provision of personal data in our possession in accordance with our procedures. We will respond within a reasonable period and scope. We may require identity verification (government-issued ID or equivalent). If fees apply, we will notify you in advance. Please contact us for details of the procedure.

12. Response to Data Breach

In the event of a personal data breach, we will identify impacts, investigate causes, implement recurrence-prevention measures, and, where required by law, promptly notify the Personal Information Protection Commission and affected individuals.

13. Voluntariness and Consequences of Non-Provision

Providing information is voluntary; however, failure to provide required information may result in inability to use part or all of the Service.

14. Compliance and Changes

We comply with applicable laws and guidelines. If we change this Policy, we will announce it on our website/admin console with advance notice for a reasonable period, commensurate with the importance of the change.
